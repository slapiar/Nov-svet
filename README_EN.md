# New World

An environment for developing human relationships, leading toward an online system, a book publication, a computer game and a film.

# Relationships, validations, goals and contracts in the New World system

## 1. Basic principle

The highest goal of the **New World** system is sustainable and life-supporting relationships between people, nature and the environment in which they live.

The system is not meant to be primarily a market, database, social network or game. It is meant to be an environment in which relationships can be recognized, established, validated, developed, protected and, when needed, formalized into goals, projects or contracts.

Every person needs something.  
Every person can offer something.  
Every need and every offer has its own context, place, time, price, degree of urgency and relationship to other participants in the system.

Therefore:

```text
relationship → demand / offer → goal → project → validation → contract
```

## 2. Relationship and session

A relationship is the basic living phenomenon of the system.

A session is a concrete technical or process trace of activity within a relationship.

A relationship can exist long-term, naturally or formally. A session is a time-bounded expression of this relationship: visiting a profile, opening a source, accepting a challenge, answering, performing an activity, making a decision or using a specific element of the system.

Therefore, these must not be confused:

```text
relationship ≠ session
session = trace of activity within a relationship
```

A session can create validations because it shows that a profile, source or element has been visited, used or activated.

## 3. Demand and offer

Relationships create the space of demand and offer.

Every participant in the system can:

* need something,
* offer something,
* search for something,
* reject something,
* accept something,
* verify something,
* create something.

Demand and offer are not only business categories. They are a basic expression of the relationship between need and ability.

```text
need → demand
ability / resource → offer
match between demand and offer → opportunity for relationship
```

## 4. Goals as opportunities for relationships

A goal gives direction to relationships.

A goal creates an opportunity for people, resources, offers, demands, authorities, validations and future contracts to gather around an idea, need or intention.

The urgency of achieving a goal also affects the value of the offer, the price of work and the priority of resources.

An offer has the highest value when it corresponds to:

* the real need of the goal,
* the right time,
* consumption limits,
* the ability to fulfil the goal,
* the verified trustworthiness of the participant.

```text
goal + time of need + resource limit + suitable offer = high relationship priority
```

## 5. Basic rights of life

The basic life-supporting legitimate requirements of living nature and the human being are:

* air,
* water,
* energy,
* food,
* knowledge,
* reproduction,
* physical health,
* mental health,
* emotional health.

These requirements are not luxury. They are the foundation of life.

The New World system must distinguish between what is necessary for the preservation of life and what is a civilizational expansion of human possibilities.

## 6. Basic human rights

A basic human right is everything a person can secure by themselves, by their own strength, consciously and without causing harm to another.

This includes especially:

* the right to life,
* the right to self-determination,
* the right to one's own body,
* the right to breath, rest and food,
* the right to a safe space,
* the right to family,
* the right to housing in an appropriate form,
* the right to seek knowledge,
* the right to carry responsibility for one's own path.

Every person lives for themselves. Nobody can eat, breathe, rest or live through another person's own decisions for them.

Therefore every person is, in principle, responsible for their relationships.

However, not every person is fully capable of assessing the quality of relationships. Therefore they need the support of wisdom, experience, intuition, community and a system of validations.

## 7. Luxury as a civilizational layer

Luxury is everything that exceeds basic necessities of life and requires services, work or resources from others.

This includes, for example:

* specialized work,
* leisure services,
* hobbies,
* culture,
* sport,
* games,
* interests,
* travel,
* higher education,
* legal protection,
* justice as an institutional service,
* civilizational comfort.

Luxury is not necessarily vanity. It is an expression of civilizational level.

An indigenous person in the jungle is subject to the laws of the jungle.  
A resident of London is subject to the laws of London.

Render unto Caesar what is Caesar's.

The system must therefore distinguish between:

```text
a basic life need
and
a civilizational service
```

## 8. Threats to human relationships

The main threats to relationships include:

* excess without measure,
* exploitation,
* slavery,
* terror,
* tyranny,
* totalitarianism,
* parasitism,
* freeloading,
* blackmail,
* criminality,
* rudeness,
* vulgarity,
* manipulation,
* abuse of trust,
* concealment of essential information.

These threats can deform relationships, devalue validations, damage contracts and lower the trustworthiness of a profile.

## 9. Validation

Validation is the confirmation of existence, acceptance, rejection, authorization or trustworthiness of a relationship, profile, resource, element, goal, project or contract.

Basic logic of validation:

```text
assigned × accepted / independently confirmed
```

Validation can have several layers:

1. assignment or offer,
2. acceptance or rejection,
3. mutual approval,
4. independent confirmation by a third party,
5. repeated confirmation by several participants of the system.

Validation can be:

* automatic,
* conditional,
* mutual,
* multilateral,
* independent,
* security-related,
* process-related,
* contractual.

## 10. Automatic and conditional validation

Where validation is needed, it must be marked in the system.

Validation is granted in two basic ways:

### Automatic validation

Automatic validation is created, for example, when:

* visiting a profile,
* opening a source,
* displaying an element,
* logging into a session,
* making technical contact with the system.

Automatic validation does not mean approval or rejection. It only confirms the existence of an event in the system.

```text
profile visited a source → a trace of the existence of contact is created
```

### Conditional validation

Conditional validation is created on the basis of a challenge, request, decision or need for confirmation.

It can take the form:

```text
1 = I accept / confirm
0 = I reject / do not confirm
```

If a participant is asked to make a decision, the system can stop them with a modal window and not allow them to continue until they take a position.

## 11. Advertising new posts

Every new post in the system, resource, profile or element should be announced to those participants to whom its existence is relevant.

Who is affected by a new element is determined according to:

* the goal,
* the project,
* authorities,
* relationships,
* validations,
* permissions,
* thematic links,
* resources,
* profile interests and responsibilities.

A new element is therefore not merely inserted into a database. It is introduced into a relationship network.

## 12. Without relationship there is no session or permission

Without an established or at least announced relationship, there is no basis for a session to arise.

A session is not an isolated event taken out of context. A session is a concrete trace of activity within a relationship. If the relationship does not exist, or if the participant has not been informed about it in any way, the system has no legitimate basis for creating a responsible session between the participant and that element of the system.

Therefore:

```text
relationship / announcement of relationship
→ session
→ permission
→ activity
→ validation
→ responsibility
```

No one may be required to take responsibility for something they do not know, are not aware of, or have not been invited into.

Therefore validation is a natural process condition of every responsible relationship.

A relationship can also be established between parties that do not know each other physically. The form of the relationship is only one of its properties.

There are relationships that are:

* natural,
* family-based,
* social,
* neighborly,
* work-related,
* temporary,
* forced by circumstances,
* political,
* project-based,
* contractual,
* spiritual,
* communal.

Not all relationships are chosen by a person. Some arise from the circumstances of life.

## 13. Project as a project-oriented solution to a goal

A project is a project-oriented solution to a goal.

The goal says what is to be achieved.  
The project says how it is to be accomplished.

```text
goal → project → activities → validations → evidence → usefulness
```

A project creates space for:

* profiles,
* authorities,
* promises,
* resources,
* tasks,
* validations,
* contracts,
* evidence,
* rewards,
* risks,
* results.

## 14. Member profile as a project of relationships

A member profile in the New World is a project of their relationships.

It is not merely a user card. The profile contains layers of:

* described past,
* imagined future,
* threats,
* risks,
* opportunities,
* goals,
* offers,
* demands,
* validations,
* authorities,
* contracts,
* evidence,
* usefulness.

Every person chooses and sets their own goals and path toward them. The system should help them recognize relationships, possibilities, risks and the consequences of their decisions.

## 15. Contract

A contract is a project-created construction of human relationships for the purpose of fulfilling a mutual agreement.

Working formula of a contract:

```text
contract = parties × demand × offer × quantity / (price × time frame × method of fulfilment)
```

A method of fulfilment can be, for example:

* one-time,
* repeated,
* continuous,
* limited by number of repetitions,
* tied to a result,
* tied to time,
* tied to validation.

A contract cannot arise without parties, subject, scope, time, price or method of fulfilment.

## 16. Authority

Authority represents permission to act or decide in a specific relationship, goal, project or contract.

Authority can include:

* permissions,
* duties,
* responsibility,
* respect,
* priority,
* wisdom,
* degree of trustworthiness.

Working formula:

```text
Auth = (Permissions × Duties × Responsibility) / Validation
```

This formula is still a working one and will need further refinement.

Authority by itself does not mean that one person is more than another person.

At the basic level, every person has a value of 1 or 0 from the point of view of a specific decision:

```text
1 = authorized / confirmed within the given scope
0 = unauthorized / unconfirmed within the given scope
```

## 17. Authority as an exponent of validity

Authority represents an exponent of validity.

Not because a person has greater human value, but because their decision can be historically more verified, more credible and less risky in a specific relationship.

Working image:

```text
validity = 1
authority = exponent of validity
1ⁿ = 1
```

The result is still the value 1 or 0. A person is neither more nor less.

The difference lies in the priority of decision where the system needs to choose an authorized authority according to credibility, experience, responsibility or legal relationship.

There is no absolute highest Authority. There is only the priority of Authority in the relationship currently being resolved.

## 18. Decision-making of authorities

If all participants of a relationship decide, the decision of every participant is important.

A single zero is enough and the relationship, contract or decision may not arise.

```text
all participants must confirm → one zero stops the process
```

If a single Authority is to decide, the system selects the one with the highest exponent of permission in the given relationship.

A project or contract can define the minimum required priority of Authority for a specific decision.

Example:

A parent of a child must have a higher authorized exponent than the child themselves in decisions about the child in areas where the law or protection of the child requires it.

If the law says that a parent or legal guardian may decide on behalf of a child, the system must be able to verify whether that profile truly has this authorized Authority.

## 19. Cluster validity

Cluster validity is created on every member profile and on every element of the system.

It is a set of validations that confirm:

* existence,
* origin,
* authorization,
* acceptance,
* scope,
* relationship,
* source,
* evidence,
* use,
* responsibility.

Validity must, where needed, be mutually confirmed and only then may it enter another relationship between two profiles, goals, projects or contracts.

The same coin cannot be lent to nine more people, as today's fractional-reserve banking system does.

By contrast, water from a well can be poured into a glass for everyone who asks, if the well confirms that it has not yet dried up.

Therefore the system must distinguish between:

```text
a scarce limited resource
and
a shareable renewable resource
```

## 20. Request, offer, demand and advertisement as a project

Every request, offer, demand, classified ad, advertisement or marketing flyer can be the subject of a project under the terms of a contract between the parties.

It is not just a message.

It is an element of the system that can create:

* relationship,
* validation,
* demand,
* offer,
* expectation,
* commitment,
* claim to a resource,
* future contract.

## 21. No one can conclude a contract on behalf of another without authorization

No one can conclude a contract on behalf of another without authorization.

Even a mother cannot force upon a child what the child does not want. She may, however, have the right to decide what the child may or may not do, if she is authorized to do so as a parent or legal representative.

A mother cannot learn, eat or live through the child's experience for the child.

Therefore parental validation is necessary for the child wherever required by law, protection of the child or system rules.

## 22. Working axiom

```text
Relationship is the foundation.
Session is a trace of activity within a relationship.
A goal gives direction to the relationship.
A project organizes the path toward the goal.
Validation verifies existence, acceptance, authorization and trustworthiness.
Authority determines permission in a specific relationship.
A contract formalizes agreement about work, resources, price, time and result.
Cluster validity protects the system from unauthorized entry, false claims and misuse of resources.
```

## Project management as the technical core for testing the system

Project management is not merely an auxiliary organizational tool, but a basic technical layer for testing relationships, goals, promises, commitments, evidence, usefulness and Authority.

The first phase of development should not aim at creating new software from scratch. Priority should be given to using existing modular solutions that allow the principles of the system to be tested in practice.

The first working candidate is the existing Zephyr Project Manager PRO installation in WordPress, especially because of its support for projects, tasks, categories, discussions, attachments, calendar, notifications, import/export and custom fields.

In parallel, open-source self-hosted solutions may also be explored, especially OpenProject, Leantime, Plane, Redmine, Vikunja and NocoBase. The goal of the comparison is not to find the richest project manager, but the tool most suitable for modeling the sequence:

```text
goal → promise → commitment → action → evidence → usefulness → Authority → relationship sustainability
```

Decisive selection criteria:

- self-hosting and control over data,
- possibility of custom fields,
- data export/import,
- API or another integration layer,
- work with roles and permissions,
- support for discussions and evidence attachments,
- possibility of gradual extension without vendor lock-in,
- usability for ordinary members, not only for developers.
